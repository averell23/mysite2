03.09.2003
Left home at 5 a.m., which should allow enough time to get to the airport and check in. The car starts acting strange after refilling fuel and motor oil. When I pull over, the motor is full of spilled oil. I decide it's probably not a good idea to continue with this car.
Luckily at this moment Mr. and Mrs. Schnaiter from the black forest are taking a break at the same rest area. They are also going to the Hahn airport for their flight, and kind enough to let me go with them. I take all the expensive stuff from the car and hope it won't be towed while I'm on holidays.
I make it to the airport just in time, which is good since Ryanaird doesn't give refunds. The rest of the journey is uneventful. Elin is picking me up in Stockholm, we take a quick stroll through the city and have some food.
04.09.2003
After breakfast I visit the lab with Elin, in order to send mail to my brother and flatmates and to let them know the status of my car.
I spend the rest of the day walking the city, take a tour of the city hall (this is where the Nobel Price dinner is held every year, among other things) and have a look at the old town.
I pick up Elin from the lab, we rent a video for the evening. Too bad there aren't any swedish movies subtitled in a language that I can read.
05.09.2003
After getting up I spend some time with my travel lecture (Haruki Murakami: The Wind-Up Bird Chronicle). Later I go to the Skansen, which is some kind of big open-air museum, where they put swedish houses of different times and regions. The houses are inhabited by folks dressed up in costumes from the era. When you enter one of the homesteads there will be a farmer inside, or the "wife" doing knitwork. They even have a small town with a church, a bakery and a post office.
At the Skansen they also have a collection of nordic animals. i I'm just right for the feeding, so I take the tour and have a look at all those wolves, bears, elks and owls. I don't make it to the aquarium, though.
In the evening we go to a mainstream techno-stylie club with some friends of Elin from the lab, but it isn't really groundbreaking.
06.09.2003
We do some shopping for clothes in Södermalm, without much success, and have a coffee at a roadside cafe. Elin has to do her washing that afternoon (obviously you have to stick to prearranged times to clean you clothes), so I return to my book.
We spend the rest of the night talking and watching evil movies from the eighties and even more evil soap operas from just now on the telly.
07.09.2003
Doing a boat ride into the Schären (the small islets just off the coast). The weather is bright, although quite cold and windy out on the water. We find a sunny spot on one of the islands, however, where you can still enjoy the sun.
After coming home we go to a restaurant for some Köttbullar (swedish meatballs).
08.09.2003
I'm going to the national museum today, which has a nice exhibition on swedish design of the 20th century. They also have a special exhibition on art with animals, which is quite good. One of the highlights is a booth with contemporary chinaware from one swedish factory. They had the same china which we used when I was in Sweden as a kid. (My father gives me one of the cups after I got home). The rest of the museum is pretty much standard fare.
Later on I sit down at the water, to get my postcards done. I also visit Elin at the lab to send off an email to Patrick in order to get some help for returning the car.
09.09.2003
Another day of museums. This time it is the technical museum, which is mildly interesting, but features to few "hands-on" exhibits for my taste. At least they have free internet terminals; I check my mail again. I still spend too much time in that museum, so I am too late for the nordic museum.
10.09.2003
Elin takes me to the bus at 3am in the morning. The journey goes smoothly, although it takes a while until to get back home on public transport. I get Patrick to tow my car to the nearest garage. It is still the same as I've left it, but has to be expensively repaired.
